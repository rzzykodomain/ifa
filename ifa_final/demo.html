Esports World Cup (EWC) - FAQ

What is the Esports World Cup (EWC)?

The Esports World Cup (EWC) is a global tournament that brings together the best teams and players across multiple esports titles to compete for championship titles and major prize pools.

When and where is the EWC held?

The EWC is an annual event hosted in Riyadh, Saudi Arabia, featuring weeks of competitions, festivals, and esports-related activities.

How can I watch the EWC?

Matches are live-streamed on Twitch, YouTube, and official EWC channels. You can also follow highlights and updates on the official website and social media.

Can I attend the event in person?

Yes! Fans can purchase tickets to attend the EWC live in Riyadh, which includes fan zones, meet & greets, and cultural showcases.

How are teams selected to participate?

Teams qualify through regional and international tournaments, invitations, or direct qualifications based on their performance.

What is the prize pool for the EWC?

The prize pool is one of the largest in esports, often exceeding $30 million USD across all titles.

Who organizes the Esports World Cup?

The EWC is organized by the Saudi Esports Federation with international partners, aiming to establish Riyadh as a global esports hub.
